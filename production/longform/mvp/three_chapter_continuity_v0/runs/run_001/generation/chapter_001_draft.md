# Chapter 001 Draft

Mara reached the first checkpoint with the access token folded inside her palm. It had gone soft at the corners because she had opened and closed her hand around it all morning.

The assessment clerk did not look up. He tapped the slate beside the left queue, then the right one, then the left again. Three taps, no explanation. The candidates ahead of Mara pretended they understood. The ones behind her pretended harder.

Lio stood near the water shelf, pretending not to wait for her. Yesterday he had covered her missed inventory count. Today he kept his sleeves down, hiding the red audit mark he had taken for it.

"Left line is faster," said Renn, the candidate beside her. "If you still have a token."

Mara looked at the clerk's slate. The left mark had a small notch beside it. It might have meant priority. It might have meant penalty. The rules board only said: one entry, one token, no reversal.

She could wait and watch three more candidates. She could ask Lio to speak for her and drag his audit mark into public view. Or she could spend the token now and keep him out of the clerk's mouth.

The clerk lifted his eyes. "Next."

Mara put the token down.

## Evidence: token-spent

The clerk pierced the token with a dull brass pin. The sound was small, but everyone in the first two rows heard it.

"Left queue," he said.

Renn smiled as if she had won something. Lio stopped pretending to drink water.

The left queue moved quickly until Mara reached the screen. Behind it sat a second clerk with a clean slate and no expression.

"Token spent," the second clerk said. "Witness required."

Mara turned before she meant to. Lio had already stepped forward.

## Evidence: ally-risk

He gave his name before she could stop him. The red audit mark showed when he raised his sleeve to sign the witness strip.

The second clerk wrote both their names. "Witness debt recorded. Next checkpoint will not accept token entry for this candidate."

Mara almost said she had not known. That would have been true and useless.

Lio did not look angry. That was worse. He only tugged his sleeve down and said, "You could have asked."

Mara kept the pierced token because the clerk pushed it back to her as proof. It was no longer useful, but it was evidence. She closed her hand around the torn corner and felt the hole the pin had made.

The first checkpoint was open. The second had already become narrower.
