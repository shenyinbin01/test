# Longform Chassis Contract

## Definition

The Longform Chassis is the non-prose structural layer that keeps a long webnovel window coherent across chapters. It preserves causality, state, debt, question, and provenance. It does not create the feeling that a character is alive.

## B Responsibilities

B is responsible for:

- `world_slice`: local world rules and constraints.
- `book_spine`: book-level promise and direction.
- `volume_card`: current window goal and bounded arc.
- `chapter_card`: chapter-level contract, hook, payoff, cost, and forbidden moves.
- `state_delta`: proposed state changes after each chapter.
- `ledger preview`: non-official preview from proposed deltas.
- `reader_question ledger`: active, partially answered, answered, delayed questions.
- `consequence ledger`: inherited costs and next-friction seeds.
- `continuity`: chapter-to-chapter inheritance.
- `drift detection`: mainline, volume goal, motivation, knowledge, relationship.
- `renderer hard boundary`: renderer must not rewrite state or causality.
- `status/provenance`: proposed vs official state discipline, evidence refs, source paths.

## B Non-Responsibilities

B is not responsible for:

- character liveliness
- scene heat
- prose feel
- author intent compression
- reader emotion decompression
- narrative stance
- de-AI surface treatment
- making dialogue or behavior feel lived-in

## Contract Sentence

B is chassis, not engine. If prose feels flat while B outputs are complete, the fix belongs to C Engine or Renderer, not to a larger B chassis.
