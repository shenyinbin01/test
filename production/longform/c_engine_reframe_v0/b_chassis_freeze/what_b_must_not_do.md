# What B Must Not Do

B must not:

- Try to make characters “alive” by adding more state fields.
- Turn relation, cost, and hook ledgers into visible prose instructions.
- Decide narrative stance or voice.
- Add scene heat by increasing conflict labels.
- Create author intent statements for the reader to see.
- Compress emotional payoff.
- Explain theme.
- Tell the renderer to use operational terms.
- Fix flat prose by expanding the chassis.
- Treat zero drift, zero anti-feed, or complete state_delta as proof of good fiction.

If a chapter is structurally valid but lifeless, B has done its job. The repair belongs to C Engine rebuild.
