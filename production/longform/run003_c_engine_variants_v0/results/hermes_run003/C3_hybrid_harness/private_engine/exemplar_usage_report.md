## Craft Exemplar 1: character_pressure

**Principle:** Protagonist chooses flawed tactic because admitting cleaner option would expose shame/debt/fear. Do not state agency. Make rejected tactic and cost visible through behavior.

### Application by Chapter:

**Chapter 001:**
- Flawed tactic: Lin Yan withholds the informal rumor from Su Tang
- Cleaner option rejected: Telling Su Tang immediately about Zhou Yan's whisper
- Why rejected: Would expose fear of being seen as someone who needs borrowed trust to advance
- Behavioral evidence: Twice opens mouth, twice closes it; looks at notice instead of at her; generic reassurance instead of real answer
- Cost visibility: Su Tang's paused pen, unasked third question, slower footsteps leaving

**Chapter 002:**
- Flawed tactic: Pushes through gate_02 alone rather than asking Su Tang for help
- Cleaner option rejected: Admitting to Su Tang that he needs her analysis of the encoded options
- Why rejected: Would require explaining why he can't decode them alone (i.e., admitting the rumor was his crutch)
- Behavioral evidence: Looks at Su Tang, wants to call out, doesn't; presses pen too hard creating ink blot; chooses B3 without information
- Cost visibility: Su Tang completes her portion efficiently while he struggles; Zhao Rui's probe lands

**Chapter 003:**
- Flawed tactic: Partial confession — tells Su Tang the factual content but not the emotional reason
- Cleaner option rejected: Full confession including "I was afraid you'd see me as someone who can't stand on his own"
- Why rejected: Full vulnerability exceeds what Lin Yan can access; his shame is too deep
- Behavioral evidence: Voice "too flat" during confession; fingers tense at his sides; cannot find the tone that carries two days of silence
- Cost visibility: Su Tang's "知道了" (acknowledged, not accepted); her look-away after his public admission

## Craft Exemplar 2: scene_heat

**Principle:** Scene heat = objective + obstacle + tactic + counter-tactic + turn + cost. Characters want different things; tactics fail; irreversible mark.

### Application by Chapter:

**Chapter 001 — The Conversation Scene:**
- Objective: Lin Yan wants to navigate gate_01 without exposing his reliance on informal signals
- Obstacle: Su Tang expects partnership and asks direct questions
- Tactic: Deflection with generic reassurances ("按流程走就行")
- Counter-tactic: Su Tang's precise follow-up question forces Lin Yan to lie by omission
- Turn: Su Tang stops asking — her silence after his answer
- Irreversible cost: Trust gap seeded; Su Tang now has evidence of withholding

**Chapter 002 — The Gate Scene:**
- Objective: Pass gate_02
- Obstacle: Triple — tactic blocked (rumor doesn't decode options), ally distant (Su Tang withholding), competitor probing (Zhao Rui)
- Tactic: Solo push through on general competence
- Counter-tactic 1: Su Tang completes efficiently, withdraws cooperation, mirrors his ch001 deflection
- Counter-tactic 2: Zhao Rui's ambiguous corridor-talk probe
- Turn: Lin Yan sees Su Tang watching him from her cubicle
- Irreversible cost: Relationship fracture visible; positional weakening; permanent uncertainty about Zhao Rui

**Chapter 003 — The Confession and Assessment Scene:**
- Objective: Pass gate_03 while managing relationship damage
- Obstacle: Gate demands visible cost; Su Tang has recalibrated to professional distance
- Tactic: Partial confession — facts without feelings
- Counter-tactic 1: Su Tang accepts information functionally but not emotionally ("知道了")
- Counter-tactic 2: Gate's structural demand — Shen Lead's direct question forces public admission
- Turn: Su Tang looks at Lin Yan after his admission, then looks away
- Irreversible cost: Notation on record; relationship crystallized at professional distance

## Craft Exemplar 3: reader_decompression

**Principle:** Prose leaves gap. Reader sees one more consequence than focal character can admit. Delay full answer when emotional cost > fact.

### Application by Chapter:

**Chapter 001:**
- Inference gap: Reader shown whisper arrives but not told its content; Reader shown Su Tang's facial shift but Lin Yan doesn't register it
- Reader advantage: Reader senses Su Tang's unease more clearly than Lin Yan does
- Decompression deferred: Rumor content and relationship cost delayed to ch002/ch003

**Chapter 002:**
- Inference gap: Reader infers rumor was circulating (via Zhao Rui's probe + Su Tang's knowing withdrawal); Lin Yan dismisses probe as fishing
- Reader advantage: Reader understands Su Tang's withdrawal as a decision about the relationship; Lin Yan sees only absence
- Decompression schedule: Partial answer (misread signal identified) now; emotional cost deferred

**Chapter 003:**
- Inference gap: Reader not told what Lin Yan unconsciously hoped Su Tang would do after his confession; must infer from his body language (looking at her, waiting a beat) and her response (looking away)
- Reader advantage: Reader sees the gap between what Lin Yan gave (facts) and what Su Tang wanted (emotional truth)
- Remaining open: The "why" of Lin Yan's silence — the emotional truth — becomes next friction

C3 successfully combines:
1. **C1 private deep fields:** All character fears, shames, misbeliefs, and private wants are compiled into natural-language renderer briefs and manifested through behavior, never stated.
