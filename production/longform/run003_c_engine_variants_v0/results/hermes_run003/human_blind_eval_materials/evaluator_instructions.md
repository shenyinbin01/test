# Evaluator Instructions — Run003 Human Blind Pairwise Evaluation

## What You Are Evaluating

You will read 12 short chapters (4 variants × 3 continuous chapters each) from a Chinese webnovel draft about an advancement assessment. The variants are anonymized (V_A, V_B, V_C, V_D). Your task is to compare them head-to-head on reading quality.

## How To Evaluate

For each pair, read BOTH variants' chapters 1-3 in sequence. Then answer these questions:

### 1. Reader Pull (读者拉力)
"After finishing chapter 3, which variant made you want to keep reading chapter 4?"

- Which variant created more curiosity about what happens next?
- Which left you with a stronger "I need to know" feeling?

### 2. Anti-AI Surface (去AI感)
"Which variant reads less like text generated by an AI?"

- Less explanatory, less summary-like
- More "a person wrote this" feeling
- Characters' internal states are shown, not explained

### 3. Character Alive (人物鲜活度)
"Which protagonist feels more like a real person with genuine desires, fears, and flaws?"

- Does the protagonist make choices that feel inevitable given who they are?
- Do they have shame, misjudgment, and private concerns beyond the plot goal?

### 4. Scene Heat (场景热度)
"Which variant's scenes have more pressure, tension, and irreversible stakes?"

- Do scenes have objectives, obstacles, tactical shifts, and real costs?
- Does each scene change something that cannot be undone?

### 5. Overall Preference (整体偏好)
"Taking all factors into account, which variant do you prefer as fiction?"

## What NOT To Evaluate

- Do NOT evaluate the assessment/world-building concept
- Do NOT evaluate how well it "solves the engineering problem"
- Do NOT prefer a variant because it seems "more systematic" or "better structured"
- Do NOT try to identify which variant is which
- Evaluate purely on: "Is this good fiction? Would I keep reading?"

## Scoring

For each pair, mark your preference. Ties are allowed but discouraged — force a choice when possible.
