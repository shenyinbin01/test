# Writer Injection Design

## Writer Inputs

Future Writer receives a compact brief, not upstream research artifacts:

```yaml
work_voice_brief:
  narrator_position:
  reader_relationship:
  protagonist_distance:
  world_attitude:
  intervention_rules: []
  rhythm_rules: []
  detail_selection_rules: []
  stable_flaws_to_keep: []
  forbidden_voice_moves: []
  anti_ai_voice_rules: []
  contamination_forbidden: []
```

The brief should appear beside chapter beat and Human Texture packet. It must be short enough that Writer can execute it without turning the draft into a checklist.

## Making The Storyteller Stable

Writer should express Work Voice through narrative decisions:

- what the narration notices first.
- how close it stays to protagonist action.
- when it withholds explanation.
- whether it treats readers as accomplices, spectators, or rule learners.
- when it lets consequence speak instead of summary.

Writer must not explain `narrator_position` or `reader_relationship` in the正文. The fields guide execution; they are not content.

## Required Execution Rules

- Do not write field names into the正文.
- Do not write any “specific author style” target.
- Do not copy source syntax, catchphrases, special nouns, or recognizable bridges.
- Do not use summary sentences instead of scene.
- Do not use emotion labels instead of emotional behavior.
- Do not explain motivation in every paragraph.
- Do not let narration read like a review report.
- Do not insert life details only to fake human texture.
- During payoff, narrator stance must follow contract: step back, stay close, or intervene at the planned timing. Random switching fails.

## Interaction With Human Texture

Writer should treat Human Texture as scene material and Work Voice as narration posture.

Example coordination:

- Human Texture says a character avoids shame.
- Work Voice decides whether the narrator names the avoidance, shows it through action, or lets reader infer it.

Writer must make both serve plot momentum. The draft should not become parallel fulfillment of two checklists.

## Avoiding Imitation And Technique-Flavor

The brief should include contamination guards in plain terms:

- no author target.
- no source work as style label.
- no source phrase.
- no source-specific world term.
- no bridge reuse.

Writer should not “perform human texture” by adding decorative habits, random concrete details, or explanatory emotional beats. Stable narrator position is not the same as more detail.

## Not Polisher-Fixable

These failures cannot be delegated to Polisher:

- no stable narrator.
- reader relationship unclear.
- scene stance contradicts contract.
- contract executed as specific author imitation.
- source contamination.
- Human Texture becomes旁白解释 instead of scene action.

Only local sentence roughness after structural approval can go to Polisher.
